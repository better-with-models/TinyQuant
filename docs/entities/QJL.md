# QJL

> [!info] Upstream method
> QJL supplies the residual-correction side of the research lineage that leads
> to TurboQuant.

## Why it matters

QJL introduces the 1-bit residual-projection story that preserves inner
products in expectation. That makes it central to the claim that low-bit
quantization can still support similarity-oriented systems.

## Relevance to TinyQuant

TinyQuant's research set uses QJL mainly as:

- an upstream theoretical anchor
- a residual-correction reference point
- a validation and comparison baseline rather than a direct implementation
  target on its own
